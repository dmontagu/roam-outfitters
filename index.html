Meet Your Guide

I've spent 23 years chasing trout across some of the world's finest waters—from the spring creeks of New Zealand to the wild rivers of Chilean Patagonia, from the mountain streams of Wyoming to the blue ribbon waters right here in Montana.

But Montana is home. Based in the Gallatin Valley, I've held my outfitter's license since 2013 and have spent decades learning the rhythms of these rivers—the hatches, the holds, the water that consistently produces.

Whether you're picking up a fly rod for the first time or you've been at this for years, I'll put you on fish and make sure you leave with a few good stories.

Waters We Fish

From legendary tailwaters to secluded private stretches, these are the rivers and lakes I know best.

Rivers

Missouri River

Craig, MT

A true trout factory—some of the highest densities per mile in the country. Premier dry fly water with diverse, prolific hatches unlike anywhere else. Big, slow-moving, and endlessly fishable. Play the game your way: streamers, nymphing, dries, or swinging a spey rod. This river lets you do it all.
